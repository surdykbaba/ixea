The framework

Three pillars for a trusted, federated ecosystem.

Africa's data is fragmented across silos, vendors and borders. IEXA sets the open rules, the trust framework, and the federation model — so that anyone can exchange data with anyone, securely and sovereignly, without rebuilding integrations every time.

How it works

Connect once. Reach the whole network.

Each member runs (or rents) a secure node — the only thing they integrate with. Through IEXA's shared registry and trust services, that single connection reaches every other participant. No point-to-point integrations, no central data store.

1

Sender

A business, bank or government agency that needs to send data.

2

Member Node

A secure gateway that signs, encrypts and routes the message onto IEXA.

3

Member Node

The receiver's node, discovered via the shared registry & trust list.

4

Receiver

The destination — paid invoice, verified identity, exchanged record.

♻️

The Once-Only Principle

Citizens and businesses should provide the same information to government once — never again. IEXA lets institutions request verified data straight from the source, with consent, instead of asking people to re-submit documents they've already given. Less paperwork, less fraud, more trust.

Why the VAT lottery matters

Fiscal "receipt lotteries" — proven in Portugal, Italy, Greece and beyond — reward consumers for requesting a real invoice, turning every buyer into a tax-compliance enforcer. IEXA bakes lottery-eligible receipt IDs into the invoice profile, so any member country can launch a VAT lottery on day one and widen the formal economy.
